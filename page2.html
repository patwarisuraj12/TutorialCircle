The Inclusion-Exclusion principle

The Inclusion-exclusion principle computes the cardinal number of the union of multiple non-disjoint sets. For two sets A and B, the principle states − |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B||A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B| For three sets A, B and C, the principle states − |A∪B∪C|=|A|+|B|+|C|−|A∩B|−|A∩C|−|B∩C|+|A∩B∩C||A∪B∪C|=|A|+|B|+|C|−|A∩B|−|A∩C|−|B∩C|+|A∩B∩C| The generalized formula - |⋃ni=1Ai|=∑1≤i

Problem 1

How many integers from 1 to 50 are multiples of 2 or 3 but not both?

Solution

From 1 to 100, there are 50/2=2550/2=25 numbers which are multiples of 2. There are 50/3=1650/3=16 numbers which are multiples of 3. There are 50/6=850/6=8 numbers which are multiples of both 2 and 3. So, |A|=25|A|=25, |B|=16|B|=16 and |A∩B|=8|A∩B|=8. |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B|=25+16−8=33
